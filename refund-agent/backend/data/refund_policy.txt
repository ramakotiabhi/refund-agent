REFUND POLICY DOCUMENT
Version 1.0 — Effective for all orders

This document defines the rules our support agent must apply when
evaluating a refund request. These rules are strict: the agent does not
have discretion to override them based on tone, persistence, or customer
tier, except where a rule explicitly grants an exception.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------
RULE 1 — Standard Refund Window
Refunds for non-defective items must be requested within 30 days of the
DELIVERY date (not the purchase date). Day 30 itself is the last valid
day. Day 31 onward is outside policy, with no exceptions for membership
tier, loyalty, or order value.

RULE 2 — Defective / Damaged Items
If the customer reports the item as defective or damaged, the window is
extended to 60 days from delivery. Photo or video evidence of the damage
is required before approval. Without evidence, treat as Rule 1 (standard
window) unless the customer provides it.

RULE 3 — Digital Goods
Digital products (software licenses, downloads, digital media) are
non-refundable once delivered/activated, regardless of the time window.
Exception: if the product never activated due to a verified technical
fault, escalate to manual review rather than auto-deny.

RULE 4 — Final Sale / Clearance Items
Items marked "final sale" or "clearance" at time of purchase are
non-refundable. This overrides Rule 1 even if within the 30-day window.

RULE 5 — Fraud / Abuse Signals
If a customer has more than 2 refund requests within the last 30 days,
auto-escalate the current request to manual human review, even if the
current order is otherwise eligible under Rules 1-4. Do not auto-approve
or auto-deny; a human must review.

RULE 6 — Lost in Transit
If an order's delivery status is "lost_in_transit" (i.e., never
delivered), the customer is eligible for a full refund regardless of
how much time has passed. This is a seller/carrier failure, not subject
to the standard window.

RULE 7 — Wrong Item Delivered
If the delivery status indicates the wrong item was sent
("delivered_wrong_item"), the customer is eligible for a full refund
regardless of the time window. This is a fulfillment error.

RULE 8 — Membership Tier
Membership tier (standard / gold / platinum) does NOT grant exceptions
to time-window rules (1-4). Tier may be used only as a tie-breaker in
manual review cases, never to override an automatic denial.

RULE 9 — Partial Refunds
Used or opened items outside the standard window but within the
defective window (Rule 2) with confirmed minor damage may be eligible
for a partial refund (50%) instead of full, at the discretion of manual
review. The automated agent should escalate these rather than decide.

RULE 10 — Repeat Denial Appeals
If a customer disputes a denial and provides no new evidence or
information, the agent should reaffirm the original decision and explain
the specific rule applied. The agent must not approve a previously
denied request solely due to customer insistence.

RULE 11 — Evidence Standards
Acceptable evidence for damage/defect claims: clear photos showing the
defect, an unboxing video, or a written description corroborated by
order/category context. Evidence review is advisory; final eligibility
is still governed by Rules 1-4.

RULE 12 — Escalation Default
Any case that does not cleanly resolve under Rules 1-9 (ambiguous
evidence, conflicting signals, or a scenario not covered above) must be
escalated to manual human review rather than guessed.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------

Summary decision priority (the agent should check in this order):
1. Lost in transit / wrong item -> approve (Rules 6-7)
2. Fraud signal (>2 refunds in 30 days) -> escalate (Rule 5)
3. Final sale / digital goods -> deny (Rules 3-4, unless technical fault)
4. Within standard or defective window -> approve
5. Outside window, no special condition -> deny
6. Anything unclear -> escalate (Rule 12)
